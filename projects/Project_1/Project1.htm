Web Site Critiques

Questions

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information? (Do they have links on the left and text on the right? Some other scheme? No scheme?)
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Website 1 Critique

Pitchfork

  1. It looks like a music magazine website. it has alot of big photos and articles. Feels modern and kind of like a newspaper format.
  2. When you open the homepage there’s a top menu with links like “Reviews,” “News,” etc. Then is shows some featured stories and the rest of the is stacked down the page.
  3. Mostly it makes sense but because there’s a lot of content it seems busy and sometimes you have to scroll a lot.
  4. The photos look good and match the style. Layout is really clean.
  5. On my computer the homepage loads fast, but it would depend on their server and also your internet connection.
  6. yes the text is easy to read. it was a good size and mostly dark text on white background.
  7. Yes there’s a navigation bar at the top with categories. Also a search icon. It stays similar across pages so thats nice.
  8. I feel it was very consistent - white background, black or dark text. The styles of reviews/news/features are the same across pages so it feels like one site.
  9. It works reasonably on phones and when you shrink the browser window. Menus collapse or turn into a different type of menu.
  10. The way the site uses bold images and strong visuals is well done. Also the filtering by genre and sections is helpful.
